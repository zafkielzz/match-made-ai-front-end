## üìù 6-Step Job Posting Process

### Step 1: General Information
**What to include:**
- Job title (be specific: "Senior Backend Developer" not just "Developer")
- Company name
- Job role category (Backend, Frontend, AI Engineer, etc.)
- Job level (Intern, Junior, Middle, Senior, Lead, Manager)
- Employment type (Full-time, Part-time, Internship, Contract)
- Required experience (e.g., "3+ years")
- Education level
- Language requirements

**Tips:**
- Use standard job titles that candidates search for
- Be honest about the level - don't inflate or deflate
- Specify exact years of experience needed

---

### Step 2: Job Overview & Description
**What to include:**

**Job Overview (2-4 sentences):**
- What team/department they'll join
- Main responsibility of the role
- Business or technical impact

**Example:**
> "Join our backend team of 8 engineers building scalable microservices for our e-commerce platform. You'll design RESTful APIs and optimize database performance, directly impacting 1M+ daily users. Work with modern tech stack and contribute to architectural decisions."

**Job Responsibilities (bullet points):**
- Design, develop, or maintain...
- Collaborate with...
- Analyze requirements and...
- Ensure performance, security, and maintainability...

**Tips:**
- Start each bullet with an action verb
- Be specific about what they'll actually do
- Mention collaboration and teamwork aspects
- Don't mix responsibilities with requirements

---

### Step 3: Candidate Requirements

**Required (Must-have):**
- Education background
- Years of experience
- Core programming languages
- Core technical skills
- Experience with specific tools/systems
- Communication/teamwork abilities

**Preferred (Nice-to-have):**
- Additional skills or technologies
- Advanced knowledge (security, performance, cloud, AI)
- Other advantages (open source, tech blog, etc.)

**Tips:**
- Be realistic - don't list everything as "required"
- Separate must-haves from nice-to-haves
- Don't ask for 5 years experience for a Junior role
- Focus on skills that will actually be used

---

### Step 4: Technology Stack

**What to include:**
- Programming languages (e.g., Python, JavaScript, TypeScript)
- Frameworks/Libraries (e.g., Django, React, Node.js)
- Databases (e.g., PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Redis)
- Tools/Platforms (e.g., Docker, Kubernetes, AWS)

**Important Rules:**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Only list technologies actually used in this role**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Don't copy-paste from other job postings**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Be specific** (not just "databases" but "PostgreSQL, Redis")
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **This section is critical for AI matching**

**Example - Good:**
```
Languages: Python, JavaScript
Frameworks: Django, FastAPI, React
Databases: PostgreSQL, Redis
Tools: Docker, AWS, Jenkins
```

**Example - Bad:**
```
Languages: Python, Java, C++, Go, Rust, JavaScript, TypeScript
Frameworks: Django, Flask, FastAPI, Spring, React, Vue, Angular
Databases: MySQL, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Redis, Cassandra, DynamoDB
Tools: Everything under the sun
```

---

### Step 5: Benefits & Working Information

**Benefits to specify:**
- Salary range & bonus structure
- Allowances (lunch, transport, phone)
- Performance review frequency
- Insurance & healthcare coverage
- Training & career development budget
- Other perks (flexible hours, gym, team building)

**Working Information:**
- Exact work location (city, district, building)
- Working hours (e.g., "Monday-Friday, 9 AM - 6 PM")
- Work mode (Onsite / Hybrid / Remote)
- Application deadline

**Tips:**
- Be transparent about salary range
- Mention all benefits, even small ones
- Specify hybrid policy clearly (e.g., "3 days office, 2 days home")
- Set realistic application deadlines

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Mixing Sections
‚ùå **Wrong:** Listing technologies in the responsibilities section
‚úÖ **Right:** Keep technologies only in "Technology Stack" section

### 2. Unrealistic Requirements
‚ùå **Wrong:** "Junior developer with 5+ years experience in 10 technologies"
‚úÖ **Right:** "Junior developer with 1-2 years experience in Python and Django"

### 3. Vague Descriptions
‚ùå **Wrong:** "Work on various projects"
‚úÖ **Right:** "Design and implement RESTful APIs for our mobile app backend"

### 4. Everything is "Required"
‚ùå **Wrong:** 20 required skills including nice-to-haves
‚úÖ **Right:** 5-7 core required skills + separate preferred skills

### 5. Copy-Paste Tech Stack
‚ùå **Wrong:** Listing every technology your company uses
‚úÖ **Right:** Only technologies this specific role will use

### 6. Hidden Information
‚ùå **Wrong:** "Competitive salary" with no range
‚úÖ **Right:** "$80,000 - $120,000/year based on experience"

## üí° Best Practices

### 1. Use Clear Language
- Write for humans, not just AI
- Avoid jargon and acronyms (or explain them)
- Use bullet points for readability

### 2. Be Honest
- Don't oversell or undersell the role
- Mention challenges as well as opportunities
- Be transparent about work conditions

### 3. Show Your Culture
- Mention team size and structure
- Describe the work environment
- Highlight learning opportunities

### 4. Make It Scannable
- Use short paragraphs
- Break up text with bullet points
- Highlight key information

### 5. Update Regularly
- Review job postings quarterly
- Update technology stack as it evolves
- Adjust requirements based on market
